# Echoes Phone — Probe Tasks (7 Days)

> **Overall theme**: Your relationship with a digital character
> **Goal**: Document how you experience the character over one week — what feels real, what breaks the spell, what surprises you
> **Daily commitment**: ~15–25 minutes

---

## Important: This is NOT a feature tour

You do NOT need to follow a strict order. **Explore the platform however you want from Day 1.** If you want to check the diary on the first hour, do it. If you never open the forum, that's fine too. The tasks below are **reflection prompts**, not instructions — they ask you to notice and document things about your experience, not to click specific buttons.

Think of this as a journal about your relationship with a fictional AI character, not a usability test.

---

## Getting Started (Before Day 1)

Set up the platform using the Getting Started Guide. Make sure:
- [ ] API connection is working
- [ ] You've imported or created a character
- [ ] You've sent at least one message and received a reply

**Submit your first reflection** via [WhatsApp/Telegram/Signal]:
> "I'm in! My character's name is [name]. First impression: [one sentence]."

---

## Day 1 — First Encounter

You'll probably explore a lot today. That's great. Go wherever your curiosity takes you.

### Reflection (submit by end of day):
1. **Screenshot**: One moment from today that caught your attention
2. **Text**: Describe your character in 3 words. Then: what made you choose those words?
3. **Text**: Was there anything that felt surprisingly "real" today? Anything that felt obviously fake?

---

## Day 2 — The Character's Private Life

By now you've probably seen some of the character's "hidden" features — diary, browser history, purchase receipts, location tracking, music taste. These are things the character doesn't know you can see.

### Reflection:
1. **Screenshot**: Something from the character's private life that struck you
2. **Text**: How did it feel to access the character's diary / browsing history / bills? Did it feel like reading someone's private journal, or like checking a game inventory? Something in between?
3. **Text**: Did any of these "private" details change how you see the character? Give a specific example.

---

## Day 3 — The Relationship

Think about the dynamic between you and the character so far.

### Reflection:
1. **Text**: Who initiates conversations more — you or the character? Does that feel natural?
2. **Text**: Has the character said anything that made you feel something genuine — happy, annoyed, moved, uncomfortable? Describe the moment.
3. **Voice note** (optional, 45 sec max): Describe the "vibe" of your relationship so far, as if explaining it to a friend.

---

## Day 4 — Identity & Expression

Think about how you present yourself to the character, and how the character presents itself to you.

### Reflection:
1. **Text**: Do you act differently with this character than you would with a human friend? How?
2. **Text**: Have you tried different chat styles (SMS / Novel / Script)? Which one felt most natural for expressing yourself? Why?
3. **Text**: Does the character have a consistent "personality"? Or does it feel random? Give an example.

---

## Day 5 — Creative Task: Character Life Map

### Task: Draw or describe your character's "life map"

On a piece of paper, in a drawing app, or in text — create a visual map of your character's life as you understand it. Include:
- Where they live
- What they do for work/hobbies
- Their relationships (real or implied)
- Their emotional state
- Anything else that defines them

### Reflection:
1. **Image**: Photo/scan of your drawing, or a text description
2. **Text**: What did you include and why? Were there gaps — things you wanted to know but couldn't find out?
3. **Text**: Did creating the map reveal anything you hadn't consciously noticed before?

---

## Day 6 — Friction & Boundaries

Think about moments where things didn't work, felt off, or where you pushed against the system's limits.

### Reflection:
1. **Screenshot**: A moment where something broke, confused you, or felt wrong
2. **Text**: Did the character ever do something that annoyed you, felt out of character, or broke the illusion? What happened?
3. **Text**: Did you try changing settings, editing memories, or customizing the character? What happened when you did — did it feel like you had control, or like you were fighting the system?
4. **Text**: If you could change one thing about how the character interacts with you, what would it be?

---

## Day 7 — Final Reflection

Last day. Have one more conversation with your character — or don't. Revisit your favorite feature, or explore something you haven't tried. This day is yours.

### Final Reflection:
1. **Screenshot**: Your final favorite moment from the whole week
2. **Text**: After 7 days, how would you describe your character to a friend? Has your description changed since Day 1?
3. **Text**: On a scale of 1–10, how "real" does your character feel? What would need to change to move that number up?
4. **Text**: If you could tell the designer one thing, what would it be?
5. **Text**: Would you continue using this after the study? Why or why not?
6. **Voice note** (optional, 60 sec max): Any final thoughts, feelings, or feedback

---

## After Day 7

### Wrap-up:
1. **Export your data** (optional): Go to Settings → Backup to export your data as a JSON file. You can keep this as a memento.
2. **Schedule your interview**: The researcher will contact you to schedule a 45–60 minute conversation. This is a relaxed chat, not a test — we'll talk about your experience using the screenshots and reflections you submitted.
3. **Return**: No physical items to return — everything is digital. Just make sure you've submitted all your reflections.

## Tips from the Researcher

- **Don't overthink it.** There are no right or wrong answers. We're interested in your genuine experience.
- **Screenshots are gold.** A single screenshot can tell us more than a paragraph. When in doubt, snap it.
- **Voice notes are welcome.** If you're more comfortable talking than typing, send a voice note instead.
- **Weird is good.** If something feels strange, unsettling, or unexpected — that's valuable data. Tell us about it.
- **You're the expert.** You know your experience better than we do. Your interpretation matters.
