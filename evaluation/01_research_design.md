# Echoes Phone — User Evaluation Research Design

## 1. Research Overview

**Project**: Echoes Phone — AI companion application for emotional disclosure, identity negotiation, and reciprocal relationships
**Method**: Digital Design Probes (Mattelmäki, 2006) + Semi-structured Follow-up Interviews
**Duration**: 7 days probe deployment + post-study interview
**Target participants**: 12–14 recruited, expect 10+ completing full cycle

## 2. Research Questions (from thesis)

**RQ1:** How can AI companionship technologies be designed to support emotional disclosure and identity negotiation while maintaining user agency?

**RQ2:** Under what interaction conditions do human-AI relationships foster genuine identity growth rather than devolving into passive, closed-world dynamics?

**RQ3:** In what ways does the design and implementation of Echoes Phone instantiate these principles to help co-creation and reciprocal relationships?

## 3. Methodology: Digital Design Probes

### Why Design Probes?

- Echoes Phone operates in a **private, emotional, subjective** space — traditional task-based usability testing would miss the point
- Probes let users **self-document their experience over time**, capturing moments of delight, confusion, discomfort, and attachment that researchers cannot directly observe
- The method aligns with the project's focus on **emotional engagement and identity negotiation** rather than functional efficiency
- Fully digital probe deployment is appropriate: all touchpoints are remote, no physical artefacts needed

### Probe Type

**Digital Empathy Probes** (Mattelmäki & Battarbee, 2002) — combining self-documentation tasks with follow-up interviews. The probe kit is a digital "assignment package" delivered via the participant's preferred messaging platform.

### Study Structure

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|-------|----------|------------|
| **Preparation** | 1 week | Finalize probe kit, recruit participants, distribute materials |
| **Probe Deployment** | 7 days | Thematic reflection tasks + daily micro-reflections |
| **Interview** | 45–60 min per participant | Semi-structured, probe-material-led |
| **Analysis** | 2 weeks | Thematic analysis, persona development, design implications |

## 4. Participant Recruitment

### Target Profile
- Age: 18–30 (primary target for AI companion/role-play interest)
- Mix of experience levels: some familiar with AI chatbots/character.ai, some newcomers
- Comfortable with English or Chinese interface
- Access to a desktop browser (Chrome recommended)
- Willing to engage with an AI character for ~15–30 min/day over 7 days

### Recruitment Channels
- University mailing lists, student Discord/WeChat groups
- Social media posts (with screening questionnaire)
- Snowball referral from initial participants

### Screening Criteria
- Has used at least one messaging app daily (familiarity with chat UI)
- No prior experience with Echoes Phone specifically
- Willing to commit to 7-day participation + one interview
- Agrees to data collection and GDPR terms

### Compensation
- €20 gift card or equivalent for completing full cycle (probe + interview)
- Partial credit (probe only, no interview): €10

## 5. Probe Kit Overview

The probe kit is a **fully digital self-documentation package** delivered via the participant's preferred messaging tool (WhatsApp / Telegram / Signal / email). It contains:

1. **Welcome & Consent** — Informed consent form (GDPR compliant), study overview
2. **Getting Started Guide** — How to access the platform, import a character, navigate the interface
3. **Task Booklet** — 7 days of thematic tasks (not feature-by-feature — users explore features freely on their own)
4. **Data Submission Guide** — How to submit screenshots, voice notes, text reflections
5. **Closing & Return** — How to wrap up, export data, schedule interview

### Probe Components (mapped to Mattelmäki's framework)

| Traditional Probe | Digital Equivalent | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Illustrated postcards | Reflection prompt cards (images + questions) | Trigger emotional responses |
| Disposable camera | Screenshot tasks + voice memo prompts | Capture moments of use |
| Theme diary | Daily check-in via messaging | Ongoing self-documentation |
| Map/drawing exercise | "Map your character's world" creative task | Visual expression of mental model |
| Collage materials | "Build a mood board for your character" creative task | Intuitive synthesis |
| Stickers/post-its | Quick emoji reactions + short text | Low-effort daily feedback |

## 6. Ethical Considerations

### GDPR Compliance
- All data stored on EU-based servers (or participant's local device)
- Data anonymized: participants identified by pseudonyms only
- Right to withdraw at any time without penalty
- Data retention: max 12 months after study completion, then destroyed
- No sharing of raw data with third parties
- If future product development may use insights, explicit secondary-use consent obtained separately

### Content Sensitivity
- Echoes Phone includes NSFW/adult content capabilities
- Participants informed that the character may generate mature themes
- Participants can set their own boundaries via the app's custom rules feature
- Interview questions designed to be sensitive to discomfort; participants can skip any question

### Emotional Safety
- Participants briefed that the AI character is fictional
- Mood check-in included in probe tasks
- Researcher available via messaging throughout the 7-day period for support
- Post-study debrief provided

## 8. Data Collection Summary

| Data Type | Source | Format |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Daily reflections | Messaging submissions | Text, voice notes, screenshots |
| Task outputs | Probe tasks (creative, exploratory) | Text, images, voice |
| Pre-study questionnaire | Google Forms / Typeform | Structured + open-ended |
| Post-study questionnaire | Google Forms / Typeform | Likert scales + open-ended |
| Interview transcript | Semi-structured interview (recorded + transcribed) | Audio + text |
| App interaction logs | Optional: anonymized usage analytics | JSON logs |

## 9. Analysis Approach

1. **Probe material review** — Organize all submissions by participant and timeline
2. **Pattern identification** — Recurring themes, emotional peaks, friction points
3. **Thematic analysis** (Braun & Clarke, 2006) — Code interview transcripts
4. **Persona development** — Create 2–3 user personas from combined data
5. **Design implications** — Map findings to specific design recommendations, evaluated against the three structural criteria for openness (genuine otherness, productive friction, ecological connection)
6. **Triangulation** — Cross-reference probe materials, questionnaires, and interviews
